# Collector Chemistry — Compare Spec

## What this is

The product specification for comparing two collector wallets.

The compare page is not a report.
It is a relationship readout between two collectors.

The core idea:

**Shared worlds, different expressions.**

---

## Purpose

The compare page should help two collectors understand:

- where their taste overlaps
- where they entered the same collections
- how they expressed themselves differently inside those collections
- which artists or categories connect them
- where their differences are meaningful
- why the overlap feels culturally interesting

---

## What this is not

It is not:
- a ranking
- a verdict
- a dating compatibility score
- a portfolio comparison
- a marketplace page
- a leaderboard

Scores or percentages may be used as orientation cues, but they should not feel like judgment.

---

## Ideal page structure

1. Result hero
2. Bridge stats
3. Shared-world spotlight
4. Exact overlap
5. Shared artists
6. Taste comparison
7. Short interpretation
8. Optional collector profile cards

---

## Result hero

The hero should make it feel like two collectors are entering the same frame.

Show:
- wallet 1 identity
- wallet 2 identity
- chemistry label
- optional percentage
- one short interpretive line

Use:
- pink/magenta accent for wallet 1
- cyan/blue accent for wallet 2
- center bridge element between them

The compare form should become visually secondary once results are loaded.

---

## Bridge stats

Bridge stats should summarize the relationship quickly.

Preferred labels:
- Shared Worlds
- Shared Artists
- Exact Matches
- Taste Signals

Avoid:
- generic metric labels
- overexplaining the scoring system
- making stats feel like a leaderboard
