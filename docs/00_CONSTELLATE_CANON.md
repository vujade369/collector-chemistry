# Constellate Canon

## What this is

This is the naming and product canon for Constellate.

It defines the larger world we are building toward, the language we use, the product promise, and the guardrails that should shape future design, copy, architecture, and implementation.

This document does not replace the existing product soul yet. It sits above it as the updated brand and product frame.

---

## Name

**Constellate**

Constellate means to form or gather into a constellation.

That is what the product does.

It takes scattered signals — NFTs, collections, tickets, memberships, places, purchases, events, objects, and other records of attention — and turns them into a readable pattern.

A wallet is not just an inventory.

It is a constellation of choices.

---

## Core idea

Every person carries a constellation of things they have noticed, kept, attended, supported, and remembered.

Constellate turns those signals into moments of recognition — online, in rooms, and inside games.

---

## Product promise

See the pattern in what you keep — and where it lights up with someone else’s.

---

## Short version

What you keep becomes a pattern.  
What overlaps becomes recognition.

---

## What Constellate is

Constellate is a social mirror for what people keep.

It helps people understand themselves through the artifacts they have collected, held, attended, supported, or chosen over time.

It then helps them see where their pattern overlaps with someone else’s.

The product starts with NFT wallets because they are public, structured, and culturally expressive.

But the long-term vision is broader than NFTs.

Constellate can eventually include:
- art
- concert tickets
- event passes
- memberships
- places visited
- purchases
- books
- restaurants
- communities
- games
- artists supported
- causes funded
- proof-of-presence
- cultural artifacts

The larger question is not:

“What is this worth?”

The larger question is:

“What does this reveal?”

---

## What Constellate is not

Constellate is not a portfolio dashboard.

It is not a financial ranking tool.

It is not a compatibility score.

It is not a social feed.

It is not a surveillance product.

It is not a way to judge whether one person’s taste is better than another’s.

Constellate should illuminate, not expose.

It should interpret, not rank.

It should reveal patterns, not reduce people to them.

---

## The two-part experience

### 1. Self-recognition

First, Constellate helps someone see their own pattern.

What did they keep?  
Where did they return?  
What worlds did they enter?  
What did they notice before they could explain why?

This is the solo profile.

### 2. Shared recognition

Then, Constellate helps two people see where their patterns overlap.

The overlap might be obvious.  
The overlap might be surprising.  
The overlap might be small but meaningful.

A shared collection.  
A shared artist.  
A shared event.  
A shared cultural signal.  
A shared object.  
A shared world.

The point is not to say two people are the same.

The point is to show where their constellations light up together.

## ARG and real-world layer

In a future ARG or live experience, Constellate can turn a wallet or cultural profile into:
- a key
- a role
- a clue
- a map
- a signal
- a pairing mechanism
- a room-building input

Examples:
- Two people with overlapping collections unlock a shared clue.
- A holder of a specific cultural artifact receives a different path.
- A room reveals its hidden constellation only after enough people opt in.
- A player’s wallet helps assign a role like Archivist, Signal Keeper, Cartographer, or Witness.

The experience should feel playful, opt-in, and human.

Not algorithmic placement.

Not profiling.

Not optimization.

More like:

“You brought a few signals with you. We used them to make the room more interesting.”

## Product vocabulary

### Constellate
The brand and overarching product.

### Your Constellation
The solo profile. The pattern formed by one person’s collected signals.

### Shared Stars
Exact overlaps. The specific artifacts, collections, artists, events, or objects two people share.

### Shared Worlds
Broader shared contexts. Collections, artists, events, places, memberships, or communities both people entered.

### Recognition Points
The surprising or meaningful similarities worth noticing.

### Crossing Points
Where two histories intersect.

### Signal Clusters
Groups of related taste, behavior, or cultural attention.

### Room Constellation
The hidden overlap map of a group, event, dinner, or gathering.

### Tap to Constellate
Future NFC or IRL interaction for instantly revealing overlap.

## Voice principles

### Recognition before analysis

The output should feel like recognition, not a report.

The user should think:

“That’s me.”  
“I didn’t realize that.”  
“Oh, you saw that too.”  
“That explains why we clicked.”

### Specific before poetic

The product can be poetic, but only after it is specific.

Do not write vague mystical language.

Do not force cosmic metaphors everywhere.

The constellation metaphor should support the product, not decorate it.

### Human before technical

Use technical systems to produce human insight.

Wallets, APIs, metadata, offers, categories, and timestamps are inputs.

The output should feel like a thoughtful read of someone’s pattern.

### Overlap, not sameness

The comparison should not imply two people are identical.

It should reveal where they share signals, worlds, or moments of attention.

Difference is also meaningful.

### Playful, not gimmicky

Constellate can feel like a toy.

It should be fun to use, surprising to share, and easy to try with someone else.

But the underlying data and interpretation must remain accurate.

---

## Guardrails

Do not center floor price, portfolio value, or financial performance.

Do not rank collectors.

Do not imply compatibility.

Do not overstate what the data can prove.

Do not expose sensitive information.

Do not make the product feel like surveillance.

Do not use cosmic language as filler.

Do not let the brand become so abstract that the user cannot tell what the product does.

Constellate should feel:
- luminous
- editorial
- minimal
- playful
- slightly mysterious
- human
- precise
- quietly premium

It should feel closer to:
- a constellation map
- a field guide
- a cultural readout
- a personal artifact
- a room map
- a social mirror

Not:
- a trading dashboard
- a leaderboard
- a generic analytics app
- a dating app
- a marketplace page
