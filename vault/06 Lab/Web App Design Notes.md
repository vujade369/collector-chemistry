# Web App Design Notes

Purpose: a short reference for translating outside web app design advice into practical Constellate design passes without copying another product directly.

---

## Concise takeaways for Constellate

- Use references to guide direction, flow, pacing, and taste. Do not copy their surfaces directly.
- Keep navigation intuitive and shallow. A user should understand where they are and what they can do next without digging.
- Make primary and secondary buttons visually consistent. Primary actions should lead attention, secondary actions should stay quieter.
- Keep touch targets at least 44x44px.
- Use 40 to 60px between major sections so the page can breathe.
- Use 16 to 24px between related elements so grouped information feels connected.
- Keep body copy readable. Favor clear type, sensible line length, and enough contrast.
- Use contrast to guide attention, not to decorate every surface.
- Keep images and icons consistent in aspect ratio so the interface feels intentional.
- NFT and card images should use useful alt text and lazy loading when possible.
- Only use visuals that add value. Remove decorative clutter that does not improve recognition, orientation, or understanding.

---

## How this applies to the profile page

- Treat the profile page as a guided reading of a wallet pattern, not a dashboard of stats.
- Let spacing create hierarchy between identity, signal clusters, holdings, and interpretation.
- Use consistent card image ratios so collections and NFTs feel comparable without implying rank.
- Make marketplace or external actions secondary unless the user clearly asks to leave the experience.
- Use readable copy and calm contrast to help users understand why a collection, creator, or overlap matters.
- Avoid visual noise. Every image, icon, badge, or button should support recognition of what the wallet keeps.

---

## Prompt rules for future design passes

- Name references by the quality they express, not as something to clone.
- Ask for shallow, obvious navigation with accessible labels and keyboard-friendly behavior.
- Specify primary and secondary button styles together, including minimum 44x44px targets.
- Specify spacing in practical ranges: 40 to 60px between major sections, 16 to 24px between related elements.
- Ask for readable body copy first, then refinement of typography and hierarchy.
- Use contrast intentionally to focus attention on the next meaningful action or insight.
- Require consistent aspect ratios for NFT, collection, creator, and card imagery.
- Require descriptive alt text and lazy loading for NFT and card images where the stack supports it.
- Cut visuals that do not clarify the wallet pattern, the collection signal, or the overlap story.
